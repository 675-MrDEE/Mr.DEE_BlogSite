Topic 1. Numbers and operations

Exercise 1 Solutions
Exercise 1 a)

Step 1. What we know: The greater than sign is saying that the number it is pointing at is smaller.

Remember  🤔

' \( 7 > 4 \)' is like ' \(7\) 👉🏿 \(4\)' ... like 7 pointing to 4 and telling him "you are smaller than me"

But is that true?
What we also know: 7 is a bigger number than 4

Step 2. Comparing what we know with what the above statement is saying: We can compare and finally conclude that the statement above is true.

Remember  🤔

1) What we know is that 7 is bigger than 4
2) What the statement is saying is, it is also saying that 7 is bigger than 4, because 7 is pointing at 4 and telling him "you are small". ... \(7\) 👉🏿 \(4\)
It is like both 7 and 4 do not know whether this is true or not and they are arguing and now they are asking us to tell them...
So looking at these two points 1) and 2), we have to compare point 2) with point 1) and since both of them are the same, that means the statement agrees with what we know and so our answer should be "True"! ... So we tell 4 sorry you are a small boy compared to 7 ...

Note: This kind of mathematical statements are called inequalities. All inequalities have a greater than or less than sign. Equations have an equal sign.



Step 3. Answer: True  ✅

Remember  🤔

Writing the answer correctly is important.
The question said "Write True or False ..." that is why we have to write "True"
That means that   \( 7>4 \)   ... means 7 is greater than 4!
